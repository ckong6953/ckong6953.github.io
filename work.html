A bit about me...

Hello! My name is Christopher Kong, and I am a rising senior at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Currently, I am majoring in both biochemistry and computer science with a minor in neuroscience. I hope to go to pursue a PhD program revolving around computational biology and/or bioinformatics. With all these goals, I aspire to work in the field of simulation of protein folding dynamics such as current projects in that field like the program Folding@Home.

At this point, I am doing research in Dr. David Lawrence (PhD)'s lab at the UNC Eshelman School of Pharmacy under the tutelage of his postdoc student Dr. Brianna Vickerman (PhD). Along with this, I am participating in the Ronald E. McNair Post-Baccalaureate Program which has helped me in discovering what research field I would be passionate about.

Aside from all that, some of my hobbies include listening to music, hiking, and programming various projects ranging from Arduino to website applications.
